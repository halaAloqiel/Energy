The Future of Energy

The energy mix of a country is the combination of different primary energy sources that make up the total primary energy supply. Our world gets our primary energy sources from burning fossil fuels. The burning of fossil fuels comes at a great cost, as it is the main contributer to global warming. Further, as the demand for energy increases, the need to shift from fossil fuels to clean energy also inceases.

Around 1/3 of global greenhouse gas emissions are from burning fossil fuels

The burning of solid fuels such as wood, crop waste, or charcoal was the dominant source of energy until the 1960s when nuclear energy was added to the mix. while solar and wind energy were added in the 1980s.

The global energy mix is still dominated by fossil fuels. They account for more than 80% of worlds energy consumption.

To reduce global emissions we need to drift away from energy systems that are depandant on fossil fuels to low-carbon sources of energy. But, as the demand for energy is growing rapidly, the speed and scale of the energy transition we need is therefore a new challenge.
