The Energy Transition

Energy mix

The energy mix of a country is the combination of different primary energy sources that make up the total primary energy supply. Our world gets our primary energy sources from burning fossil fuels. The burning of fossil fuels comes at a great cost, as it is the main contributer to global warming. Further, as the demand for energy increases, the need to shift from fossil fuels to clean energy also inceases.

Around 1/3 of global greenhouse gas emissions are from burning fossil fuels

The burning of solid fuels such as wood, crop waste, or charcoal was the dominant source of energy until the 1960s when nuclear energy was added to the mix. while, solar and wind energy were added in the 1980s.

The global energy mix is still dominated by fossil fuels. They account for more than 80% of worlds energy consumption.

To reduce global emissions we need to drift away from energy systems that are depandant on fossil fuels to low-carbon sources of energy. But, as the demand for energy is growing rapidly, the speed and scale of the energy transition that we need is therefore a new challenge.

Renewables mix

As part of vision 2030, Saudi Arabia vows to increase the renewable mix to 50% in 2030 and reduce the amount of liquid fuel. The vision was first announced in 2016, during that time, only 0.1% of the electricity mix came from renewables. To achieve this goal, His Majesty the King has launched The National Renewable Energy Program. The National Renewable Energy Program presents new oppurtunities for growth, as it would introduce a new industry for renewable energy technology. Further, The King Abdullah City for Atomic and Renewable Energy are strategizing a plan that would intruduce the use of atomic energy in the mix. A diverse energy mix would benefit the economy and environment, maximize the upkeep of petroleum resources for future generation, and provide more power for desalination.

vision 2030 target: (40GW of Solar PV, 16GW of wind and 2.7GW of concentrating solar power).
